SaaS Conversion Funnel & Attribution

Client: WinningHunter · Feb–Mar 2026

🎯 Business Problem

WinningHunter, a SaaS product, had no visibility into where users came from, how they moved through the conversion funnel, or which traffic sources were actually generating revenue. Marketing spend was flying blind.

🔧 Methodology

  • Configured Google Tag Manager (v33) with 6 custom events: Landing, Signup Started, Payment, Thank You, form_start, successful_subscription
  • Built a 4-step conversion funnel in GA4 (Landing → Signup → Payment → Thank You)
  • Created attribution explorations: Revenue by Traffic Source filtered by successful_subscription event
  • Analyzed user behavior by city, device category and traffic source
  • Delivered a full Looker Studio dashboard with funnel visualization, drop-off analysis, and attribution tables

💡 Key Findings

  • The critical bottleneck is Signup → Payment: 97.7% abandonment rate — the largest drop in the entire funnel
  • Google (organic + paid) drives 55.6% of successful subscriptions; direct traffic accounts for 34.7%
  • Desktop accounts for 89.5% of all events; product is primarily used on desktop
  • Top cities by engagement: Istanbul, Amsterdam, Kuala Lumpur, Rotterdam, Baltimore
  • /winning-products-finder has a 69% bounce rate — highest on the platform, flagging a UX issue

📌 Business Recommendations

  • Investigate and optimize the Signup → Payment step to reduce 97.7% abandonment — this is the highest-leverage improvement available
  • Double down on Google channels (organic + paid) as primary revenue drivers
  • Fix /winning-products-finder UX to reduce 69% bounce rate
  • Build a mobile-first experience: only 10% of traffic comes from mobile, suggesting potential untapped audience

Brazilian E-Commerce Analysis

Olist Public Dataset · June 2025

🎯 Business Problem

Olist, Brazil's largest e-commerce platform, needed a comprehensive analysis of sales performance, customer behavior, logistics efficiency, and payment patterns across all Brazilian states to identify growth opportunities and operational weaknesses.

🔧 Methodology

  • ETL in Excel: Power Query cleaning — null/blank handling, column merging via primary keys, date/currency format normalization
  • SQL analysis: Imported 9 CSV tables, defined PKs/FKs, created stored procedures for monthly sales by payment type, triggers for cancellation flagging and late delivery tracking
  • EDA: Descriptive statistics (mean, median, std dev, skewness, kurtosis, IQR) across 8 business dimensions: Sales, Customers, Payments, Products, Sellers, Geography, Reviews, Temporal
  • Visualization: 8 dual dashboards (Excel + Power BI) with interactive slicers by year, payment type, and order status

💡 Key Findings

  • São Paulo dominates all metrics: 41,746 customers, $599M spend, 1,849 sellers, $1.02B seller revenue, fastest deliveries (≤9 days)
  • Credit card is the primary payment method: 76,795 transactions, $1.25B in sales
  • Health & Beauty is the top-selling category at $125.8M; Furniture & Decor leads in shipment weight at 7,993 tons
  • Sales grew 14,700%: from $4.97M (2016) to $738.6M (2018)
  • 56% of all reviews are 5-star; delivered orders receive far more positive reviews than undelivered
  • High skewness (3.86–4.50) across sales variables confirms extreme regional concentration

📌 Business Recommendations

  • Optimize logistics in SP to reduce freight costs ($71.8M) and expand pickup-point network
  • Promote installment payments in low-demand states to increase average order value
  • Address Sports & Leisure cancellations (51 cancellations) — investigate product quality and delivery
  • Expand seller network in underperforming regions with targeted onboarding incentives
  • Launch seasonal campaigns (Children's Day, local holidays) to capture regional demand spikes

Coffee Bean Sales — Profitability & Customer Segmentation

Kaggle Public Dataset · May 2025

ExcelPower BISQLPower QueryEDASegmentation
1,000+ Coffee Orders Analyzed
3 Countries Covered
$470K Liberica Total Revenue
13% Liberica Profit-to-Price
2019–22 Time Range
4 Coffee Types Analyzed

🎯 Business Problem

A coffee distributor needed to understand which coffee types and roast levels were truly profitable (not just most sold), which customer segments offered the best lifetime value, and how to optimize their product mix across the US, UK, and Ireland markets.

🔧 Methodology

  • Excel ETL: Power Query cleaning, custom columns (Profit Margin, Profit-to-Price Index), null handling for email/phone fields
  • SQL analysis: Stored procedures for monthly/type-based sales, joins across orders/customers/products tables
  • EDA: Statistical analysis (mean, median, std dev, skewness, kurtosis) on Unit Price, Profit, Profit Margin, Quantity
  • Dashboards: 3 dashboards (Consumption Trends, Prices & Profitability, Customer Clusters) in both Excel and Power BI

💡 Key Findings

  • Liberica is the most profitable coffee — not the most sold. 13% return per dollar vs. Robusta's 6%
  • Arabica leads in volume (947 units) but is less efficient in profit per dollar
  • Light Roast is the most popular overall; US customers strongly prefer it
  • US market dominates with 2,760 units sold and 371 loyalty card holders vs. 81 (Ireland) and 27 (UK)
  • 2021 was the peak profitability year (32% margin); 2022 saw a sharp decline to 16%
  • Unit price ranges from $2.69 to $36.46 — highly varied price structure with right-skewed distribution (skewness: 0.99)

📌 Business Recommendations

  • Promote Liberica in premium formats (capsules, cold brew, limited editions) to capitalize on its 13% profit efficiency
  • Expand Arabica + Light Roast availability as the top demand combination
  • Invest in loyalty programs for UK and Ireland — loyalty card penetration is critically low
  • Investigate 2022 profit drop — potential causes: cost increases, pricing erosion, or competitor entry
  • Develop cross-sell bundles (coffee + complementary products) to increase ticket size for small-format buyers

Automated Email Data Extraction

n8n + Power Automate · Google Sheets · March 2026

n8nPower AutomateOpenAI APIGoogle SheetsGmailAutomation
2 Automation Workflows Built
200 Rows Auto-Generated
0 Manual Data Entry
5 Fields Extracted per Email
AI OpenAI Agent for Parsing
Real-time Trigger-based Execution

🎯 Business Problem

Organizations receive high volumes of emails containing structured information — patient records, invoices, registrations, leads — that must be manually copied into spreadsheets. This process is slow, error-prone, and doesn't scale. The challenge was to build a fully automated pipeline that extracts key fields from incoming emails and logs them into a structured format with zero manual intervention.

🔧 Solution 1: n8n + OpenAI Agent

  • Trigger: Manual execution fetches batch of Gmail messages via the Gmail node
  • Edit Fields node structures the raw email payload (Date, Name, Email, Subject, Message)
  • An AI Agent powered by OpenAI GPT parses and normalizes each email body — extracting entities, cleaning formatting, and standardizing values
  • Edit Fields2 maps the agent output to the target schema
  • Append Row node writes structured data to Google Sheets automatically
  • Get Rows confirms 200 rows successfully written after execution

🔧 Solution 2: Power Automate Flow

  • Trigger: "When a new email arrives" (Outlook V3) — fully real-time, no manual execution needed
  • Get Email (V2) fetches the full email body
  • Convert HTML to Text step cleans rich-text formatting
  • Sequential Compose actions extract: Received Time, Patient Name, Patient Address, Aide Name, and Date
  • Output is written to Excel Online row-by-row on each new incoming email

💡 Key Findings & Impact

  • Both workflows achieve 100% hands-free data extraction — no human copy-paste required
  • The n8n + AI Agent approach handles unstructured email bodies where field positions are inconsistent
  • The Power Automate flow excels for real-time, structured emails (like patient care notifications) where fields are predictable
  • Google Sheets output achieved 200 rows in a single execution from 10 email batches
  • Demonstrates practical use of AI + automation for operational data pipelines, not just reporting
